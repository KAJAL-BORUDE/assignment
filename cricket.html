CRICKET

    • cricket sport
    Cricket is a bat-and-ball game that is played between two teams of eleven players on a field, at the centre of which is a 22-yard (20-metre; 66-foot) pitch with a wicket at each end, each comprising two bails balanced on three stumps.
    rules of cricket
  • Here are some rules of cricket:
  • Teams: Two teams of 11 players compete against each other.
  • Field: The game is played on an oval or circular ground with a rectangular pitch in the middle.
  • Wickets: Two sets of three sticks, called wickets, are placed at each end of the pitch
  • Bails: Horizontal pieces called bails sit on top of each wicket.
  • Turns: The teams take turns batting and bowling, which are called innings.
  • Scoring: The batting team scores runs by hitting the ball and running to the other end of the pitch.
  • Fielding: The fielding team tries to dismiss the batters by catching shots, hitting the wickets, or using LBW.
  • Getting out: A batter is out if a fielder hits their stump while they are outside the crease.
  • Walls: A run is awarded if the ball hits the side walls or the wall behind the wicket-keeper.
  • Winning: The team with the most runs at the end of the game wins
